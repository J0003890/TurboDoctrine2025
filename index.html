Our Vision

We know that in many areas we are still far short. But we are determined to do our very best to create such an organization. To make this happen, we need a clear, mutual understanding of how we operate. Building a great organization is never the job of any one person—it's a collective effort from everyone. This doctrine is our shared set of rules and beliefs, a guide to how we behave and work together to make TURBO a place where each and every one of us, flourishes.

At TURBO, we see our organization as a place where professionals come together to achieve something meaningful. Our relationships are built on professionalism—respect, trust, and a shared drive to succeed, forged by shared journeys. This bond demands that we hold each other to high standards, as that's how we achieve great things collectively. Personal friendships are embraced, but they should never compromise our professional standards.

We strive to create a workplace that feels like a family—a supportive, connected team driven by shared objectives. While we are committed to helping each other, we could not tolerate negative behavior, laziness, or consistently incompetence, because these undermine everyone's morale and our collective success. By blending the warmth and support of a family with a steadfast commitment to excellence, we ensure every team member brings their best while having a happy workplace.

We understand that workplace satisfaction is vital. High employee turnover imposes significant costs on any organization. When talented professionals feel fulfilled and choose to stay and build their careers over the long term, it creates compounding benefits. For TURBO, we mitigate those turnover costs, retain crucial knowledge, foster deeper collaboration through established trust, and maintain momentum on long-term challenges.

For the individual, sustained commitment within a challenging environment like ours offers the invaluable opportunity to do and own something from conception through to tangible results, building a portfolio of impact. Executive search insights and leadership studies often observe that paths to senior positions usually require demonstrated long-haul strategic impact and building deep organizational trust—a path that is extremely difficult for those who frequently change jobs.

Together, we will create a place where dedicated professionals do meaningful work, grow relentlessly, and deliver lasting impact.

Our Core Beliefs

These core beliefs define how we operate and what guides our decisions:

Operate as a True Meritocracy

Opportunity, recognition, and advancement are based purely on merit – demonstrated skills, performance, and contribution. We do not engage in favoritism. Consistent with this, we do not implement Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) programs, as we believe they conflict with evaluating individuals strictly on their merits. We achieve fairness by rewarding capability and results alone.

Focus on Impact Over Titles

Your contribution and the results you achieve are paramount. We value the tangible difference you make far more than your formal title.

Prioritize Technical Excellence & Individual Contribution

We deeply value the expertise of our individual contributors – the engineers, builders, and thinkers who execute the work. Our goal is to maximize the ratio of individual contributors to managers, recognizing that expertise is our engine for progress.

Ensure a Flat Structure

We deliberately minimize organizational layers. A shorter chain of command enables faster decisions and more direct action.

Commit to In-Person Collaboration

We are a full-time, in-office workforce. We believe working together physically is essential for building a strong culture, fostering collaboration, and facilitating learning. Exceptions are strictly limited, temporary, and granted only in rare circumstances.

Hire for Skills and Drive

Our hiring decisions focus on capability, intrinsic motivation, cultural alignment, and proven results (like work samples). We value demonstrated ability and a 'doer' attitude over presentation style.

Cultivate Internal Growth

We prioritize hiring individuals with high potential and investing in their development within TURBO. Experience shows us that cultivating our own talent yields the best long-term results compared to relying heavily on external senior hires.

Emphasize On-the-Job Learning

Development happens primarily through tackling challenging work and learning from practical experience, with guidance from managers and peers. We support self-learning by providing resources and context, but we do not mandate formal training hours or 'spoon-feed' development. Progress is assessed based on capability growth.

Be a Workplace, Not Primarily a School

While learning and growth are crucial outcomes we support, our primary purpose is achieving impactful results through meaningful work. Learning is an integral byproduct, not the sole objective.

Prioritize Loyalty: Non-Rehiring Policy

Our firm policy is not to rehire former employees, ensuring fairness to our loyal team members who remain committed to the company. While very rare exceptions may have occurred historically, going forward, the only potential exceptions are for those who left solely for advanced studies or critical family caregiving responsibilities, and were not otherwise employed during that period. Our primary focus is investing in and rewarding our dedicated current employees.

Absolute Zero Counter-Offers: Loyalty Has Value

Our policy is absolute: we have never made counter-offers, and we never will. We believe loyalty has tremendous value. Counter-offers undermine this; they reward active job seeking and devalue the commitment shown by our dedicated team. It is our job to ensure our people are rewarded fairly and proactively, well before they feel the need to look elsewhere. Therefore, our financial rewards and recognition are directed towards our loyal employees – those invested in our shared future – not towards reactive bids for individuals exploring external options.

Practice Direct & Constructive Feedback

We believe in honest and direct communication. Consistent high performance may naturally result in less feedback. If performance issues arise, we will address them constructively and transparently, always aiming for improvement. Should separation become necessary after genuine efforts, the process will be handled with fairness and respect, considering individual circumstances.

Admit We're Not Perfect & We Need to Talk

Let's be honest – we strive to do great work, but we'll inevitably make mistakes or implement things imperfectly. Recognizing this, we believe the only way forward is together, through open conversation. If you see an issue, or if something makes you unhappy, please bring it up. We genuinely want to hear it, listen respectfully, and work together to address it.

True Open Door Policy: Talk to Anyone

We operate a genuine open door policy. If you have any problem, concern, or idea, we encourage you to bring it forward. You are encouraged to speak with anyone you feel comfortable with, regardless of their role or level – not just your direct manager. Everyone, including the CEO, is accessible and ready to listen. If your issue is urgent, please let us know, and we will prioritize addressing it promptly.

Ensure a Safe Workplace

We are committed to maintaining a physically and emotionally secure environment. There is zero tolerance for harassment or bullying in any form.

Respect Personal Privacy

We respect the boundary between professional and personal life. An employee's personal life is their own, unless it begins to directly and negatively impact their work or the collective workplace environment.

Offer Responsible Unlimited Leave

We provide unlimited paid time off because people have different needs throughout each period of their life, trusting our team to use it responsibly and with consideration for colleagues and work commitments.

The TURBO Way

At TURBO, our culture is our competitive advantage, forged through relentless focus on what matters and an unwavering commitment to excellence. These five principles aren't mere suggestions—they are the foundation of everything we do. They define how we work, collaborate, and win together in our pursuit of extraordinary results. While these principles form our foundation, they serve as general guidelines; specific roles may require less of certain principles. For example, roles demanding extreme accuracy, like in certain operations, will naturally temper principles such as 'Act Now, Fix Later' with appropriate caution.

1. ONE TURBO

Teamwork isn't optional. No single person, no matter how talented, has ever achieved greatness alone. When we combine our diverse skills, innovative ideas, and perseverance, we make the impossible possible. It's about collective force, not individual recognition.

  • Work Together
  • Celebrate Teams, Not Individuals
  • Share Knowledge Always
  • Blow Up Departments
  • Open Information

2. TRUTH AND TRUST

Trust makes everything faster and stronger. Honesty eliminates waste, speeds decisions, and prevents catastrophic failures. When transparency becomes our foundation, we are ready to forge ahead to solve any confronting challenge.

  • Do What You Say
  • Give Direct Feedback
  • "I Don't Know" Shows Strength
  • It's OK to be Wrong
  • Fairness, Always

3. EFFECTIVE, SIMPLE, AND PRACTICAL

Only do things that matter. Eliminate unnecessary complexity. Focus on what matters, execute precisely, and simplify ruthlessly. Complexity slows down progress. We solve real-world problems with tangible results, avoiding overengineered solutions.

  • First, Ask Why?
  • Obsess on Effectiveness
  • Delete, Then Simplify
  • Hands-On
  • Collaborate More, MeetingLess

4. SPEED AND IMPACT

We act now, fix later, making bold decisions with 80% of the information and adjusting as we go, prioritizing momentum over perfection. Success is measured by outcome, not hours. By maintaining a laser focus on what matters, we direct our energy into critical priorities, avoiding the pitfalls of scattered efforts.

  • Act Now, Fix Later
  • Outcome, Not Hours
  • Laser Focus on What Matters
  • Work Smart AND Hard
  • Solve the Unsolvable

5. GRIT AND GROW

Success will never be handed to you—it's earned through grit, growth, and relentless effort. In a world obsessed with quick fixes and passive consumption, real progress demands hard work. Reading and listening aren't enough; true learning happens when you roll up your sleeves and dive in.

  • Grit Over Genius
  • Depth Through Practice
  • Better Every Day
  • Leaders Do the Work
  • Learn Forever

Our Expectations at TURBO

We all contribute to making TURBO an outstanding place to work and succeed. Achieving our collective goals requires clarity on what is expected from each of us, reflecting our diverse roles. Meeting and exceeding these expectations are essential for career advancement and promotion to the next level within the company.

Expectations for Everyone

As a member of TURBO, you represent the company and contribute to our shared mission. We expect everyone to embody the following principles daily:

1

Embrace the TURBO Way

Our collaborative culture is central to our success. Operate as one unified team—Team TURBO—ensuring your actions benefit the company as a whole. Collaborate effectively with colleagues, generously share skills, resources, and ideas, and always prioritize the team's collective success.

2

Operate Professionally & Take Ownership

Demonstrate responsibility and accountability for your work and its outcomes. Address challenges proactively and with determination, pushing tasks through with a high standard of excellence.

3

Maintain a Positive Attitude

Approach challenges with a proactive, "can-do" spirit, focusing on finding solutions. Foster a positive and supportive environment for your colleagues, actively discouraging negativity, gossip, or undermining behavior.

4

Provide Feedback

We value transparency and continuous improvement. If aspects of your work, team, or company environment are causing concern, share your feedback constructively directly (directly with leadership, including the CEO). Open dialogue allows us the opportunity to understand and address issues. Proactive communication about concerns is expected so we can work towards solutions together.

Success Indicators:

  • You become a recognized resource, often sought out by others for your expertise or assistance.
  • You demonstrate capacity and willingness to take on increasing responsibility, sometimes extending beyond your immediate role.

Additional Expectations for Senior Roles

As a senior professional at TURBO, whether leading people or complex initiatives, you are a leader influencing your peers, project outcomes, and the broader company culture. In addition to the expectations for everyone, we require individuals in these senior roles to:

Be a Reliable Go-To Person

Serve as a dependable resource and source of expertise for colleagues across the organization within your domain. Foster trust and be sought out for guidance and support.

Drive Results and Progress

Actively propel projects, initiatives, or teams towards achieving ambitious goals and delivering excellent results. Continuously identify opportunities for improvement and uphold high standards of execution and quality within your sphere of influence.

Initiate Solutions Proactively

Identify challenges and opportunities within your area of responsibility or technical domain and take ownership to develop and implement effective solutions without waiting for direction.

Ensure Fairness and Objectivity

Treat all colleagues impartially and equitably in your interactions and collaborations. Contribute to objective decision-making and foster an environment of mutual respect.

Maintain and Leverage Deep Expertise

Possess and continuously develop the necessary domain knowledge and technical/functional competence. Effectively apply this expertise to solve complex problems, contribute to strategic solutions, make informed decisions, and mentor others where appropriate. This deep competence is fundamental to success in your senior role.

Additional Expectations for Senior Leadership & Executives

As an executive leader, you are entrusted with setting TURBO's strategic direction and shaping its future. Beyond the expectations for all employees and managers, we look to you to:

Strategic Foresight

Think critically about the future, anticipate market shifts and emerging trends, and proactively prepare the organization for upcoming challenges and opportunities.

Champion a Clear Vision

Maintain a holistic view of the organization, prioritizing TURBO's long-term success in all decisions. Clearly articulate the company's direction and strategic priorities.

Lead Innovation

Pioneer new initiatives and guide the company into new strategic territories. Demonstrate original thinking and effective leadership, particularly on novel or complex projects.

Financial Acumen

Possess a strong understanding of financial principles and company performance metrics. Make fiscally responsible decisions that support TURBO's sustainable growth and stability.

Positive Outlook

Steadfastly champion a positive, determined perspective throughout the organization, even during challenging times. Foster a culture of resilience and belief in the company's mission.

Broad Experience

Continuously seek to broaden your understanding and experience across different functional areas of the business. Unless deep technical specialization is core to your role, expect and embrace opportunities for role rotations every few years to enhance strategic perspective.

Our Performance Management Philosophy

Core Principles

Performance Management

We evaluate performance based on impact and results, not solely on effort. While we do recognize exceptional effort, particularly when overcoming challenges, outcomes are the primary driver of assessment and recognition. We reward setting ambitious goals ("aiming high") rather than defaulting to easily achievable targets. To ensure transparency and alignment, goals (including the CEO's) are public, clarifying our collective objectives.

Promotion

Advancement at TURBO is driven by impact. We promote individuals who demonstrably enhance the company's success through significant value creation, continuous skill development, proven dependability, and effective leadership (where applicable). We actively seek to elevate high-impact contributors into roles where they can achieve greater results and maximize their contribution to the company.

Compensation

Your total compensation reflects your value, contribution level, and overall impact at TURBO, considered alongside the company's financial performance. We ensure that higher performance and broader impact translate to higher compensation, enabling us to attract, retain, and reward top talent. Roles demanding greater technical complexity, wider strategic context, broader scope, increased responsibility, and more significant impact generally command higher compensation, reflecting the demanding nature and value of these contributions.

Compensation Structure & Seniority

The structure of compensation changes with seniority. Specifically, the proportion of variable pay (such as bonuses) relative to base salary typically increases at higher levels. This means senior positions offer greater upside potential linked to company success but also carry more downside risk during challenging periods, aligning senior incentives more closely with overall business performance compared to junior roles.

Compensation Structure & Role Family

The compensation mix also varies by role family. Roles with a more direct influence on key business outcomes (like revenue generation or profitability) typically have a larger variable compensation component relative to their base salary. Consequently, in strong performance years, these roles often see a proportionally larger increase in variable pay. Conversely, during challenging years, the variable component for these roles may decrease more significantly. For instance, variable pay for sales or business development roles often fluctuates more year-to-year than roles with less direct impact on immediate financial results, such as administrative positions.

Compensation Distribution and Timing

Our goal is to provide a more consistent and predictable compensation flow for employees throughout the year. We are modifying our pay practices to distribute earnings more evenly, rather than concentrating significant amounts into infrequent periods (like large annual bonuses). This approach aims to provide employees with a more stable income stream, allow for early recognition of company success and individual contributions, and mitigate retention risks associated with single, large payout dates, benefiting both our team members and the company's stability.

Our Compensation Framework

General Compensation Components

Base Salary

This is your stable, fixed compensation for executing your role effectively. It increases based on your sustained contributions, skill growth, performance trajectory, and the overall success of the company.

Position Allowance

This is the allowance tied to a certain specific position or job level.

Incentives

Certain roles, particularly those with directly measurable outputs like Sales, may have specific incentive plans tied to achieving predefined targets. These incentives typically form part of the variable compensation package.

Bonus

This is variable compensation designed to reward strong performance at the individual, team, and company levels. The total bonus pool is directly determined by TURBO's financial performance using a transparent framework.

Bonus Structure

Advance Bonus Payment

A small fraction of the anticipated annual bonus will be prepayed out monthly throughout the year. This amount is calculated based on company performance from two quarters prior and is typically a percentage of base salary. Eligibility requires a qualifying performance rating (B rating under current scale or 7 or B- under new scale) from the preceding performance cycle; new hires without a rating are not eligible until they receive a provisional rating after 6 months.

Year-End Bonus

The remaining portion of the annual bonus (after accounting for any Advance Bonus payments and applicable incentives paid). This is typically paid in six monthly installments from March to August following the performance year. Its size reflects individual, team, and overall company performance for that year. This component is highly variable: it will be substantial in strong financial years but may be significantly reduced or even zero if the company experiences poor results or financial loss.

Equity Awards (Stock Grants/Options)

Long-Term Compensation

Equity awards are a significant component of variable compensation, designed to recognize and reward sustained, impactful contributions to TURBO over the long term. Consistent with our overall compensation philosophy, the proportion of total variable compensation delivered through equity awards typically increases with position level and seniority.

Equity Grant Timing

Our intention includes an initial, significant-scale grant anticipated shortly before a potential IPO, followed by periodic smaller scale grants thereafter. The specific timing of this first round, occurring a few days prior to the potential IPO, is influenced by regulatory considerations.

For this initial pre-IPO grant, award sizes will primarily be determined by a combination of factors, including the employee's start date (with earlier tenure generally resulting in much larger grants) and the strategic impact associated with their role. This round also aims to formally grant awards to all remaining participants under TURBO's equity incentive, fulfilling our longer-than-expected outstanding commitments. Future periodic grants will continue to incentivize ongoing performance and long-term commitment.
